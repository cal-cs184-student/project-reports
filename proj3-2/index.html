Differences between pinhole and thin-lens camera models

The pinhole camera model and the thin-lens camera model are two different ways of modeling how light travels through a camera.

Pinhole camera

  • Assumes that light travels in straight lines
  • Image is formed by the intersection of these lines with the image plane
  • Creates an upside down image
  • Assumes that the hole is very small, thus the image is not blurry at all.

Thin-lens camera

  • Assumes that light travels through a lens and is refracted, or bent, as it passes through the lens
  • Image is formed by the convergence of these refracted rays onto the image plane
  • Further assumes that the lens is thin, meaning the rays going through the lens can converge at a single point. This is not achievable practically since material has thickness but it is achievable in our assignment.
